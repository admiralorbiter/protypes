Birthday Paradox

In a group of n people, the chance that at least two share a birthday grows surprisingly fast. This page compares the exact probability with a Monte Carlo simulation.

Results

    Theory

    Probability of no shared birthdays: Πk=0..n-1 (365 − k)/365. The paradox is how quickly the complementary probability 1 − Π(...) approaches 0.5; by n = 23 it’s about 50%.
